Operators

Addition

The basic principle of maths consists of Addition, which is adding two or more numbers together. For example, if we have three apples and bought two more we're going to add it to what we already have, so three plus two is five apples.

Subtraction

The basic principle of maths consists of su, which is minusing two or more numbers together. For example, if we have three apples and sold two we're going to subtract it to what we already have, so three minus two is one apple.




Multiplication

The basic principle of maths consists of Multiplication, an example of this would be if we were given two boxes of three apples in each box, we would multiply three by three to get the total apples.

Division

The basic principle of maths consists of division, an example of this would be if we had one-hundred apples and we needed to put them in boxes of five apples, the amount of boxes we need would be one-hundred divided by five, which is twenty.

Maths Models
Deterministic One input and One output
Statistics Involves Model building, inferential statistics and Descriptive statistics

What is Model building, inferential statistics and Descriptive statistics?

  • Model building involves creating Regression Lines and predicting.

  • inferential statistics involves Population, sample, parameter and statistics. Population is everyone, a sample is a chosen amount of people from a population. If you're measuring the sample it would be called a statistics, otherwise measuring the population would be called a parameter.

  • Descriptive Statistics are all visualisations such as barcharts, histograms etc...

There are two data types, Qualitative and Quantitative.

Data Types
Qualitative An attribute that belongs to a certain person. For example, ID number.
Quantitative Is mostly numerical but in some cases it is not but records the amount of something.

Thorough details on Qualitative and Quantitative?

  • Qualitative can be either ordinal or nominal; ordinal is when its ordered usually by rank.

  • Quantitative can be discrete or continious; discrete is when its a natural number and continious means its any real number.
